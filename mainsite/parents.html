New College Orientation 2014



For Parents


What is Frosh Week?

Frosh Week is the orientation programming that happens the week before the academic year starts at the University of Toronto. It is designed to introduce your child to university life, academically, socially, and culturally. Whether it’s making new friends, meeting professors, or chatting with the registrar – we’ve got it covered. This combination of academic, health, and social events will help make your child’s transition to university life a smooth and successful one.

Safety and Adult Supervision

Every event has been carefully designed to ensure student safety. For every group of 15 incoming students, there will be 2 frosh leaders (upper year students who will lead your child through the week). Our first aid team is comprised of over 40 students with CPR level-C certification, 5 trained Emergency First Responders, and one trained Emergency Medical Responder. In the event of an emergency or injury, we have personnel who are fully equipped and trained to handle situations ranging from overcrowding, to burns and lacerations, to childbirth. ORSL (The Office of Residence & Student Life), which is comprised of faculty members, is our partner in making sure that Frosh Week is a safe and accident-free experience for all students.

Why should my child attend Orientation?

Frosh Week is the perfect chance for your child to get to know his/her home away from home for the next four years. By attending Frosh Week, your child will get a head start on becoming comfortable at university. Throughout the week students will be split into groups of roughly 15 students with two upper year student leaders. During the week, students will be acquainted with the campus, have the opportunity to hear professors and faculty staff speak, and meet their classmates. Our programming takes into account that many of our students are commuters and/or new to Toronto and provides them with the information that they need to feel comfortable in the city.

Will there be food at events?

Yep! We will be providing lunch and dinner on most days, with only a few exceptions. Each day’s activities begin at 9am or later, leaving time for students to eat at home before their commute or in the dining hall if they are living on residence. Our schedule will be updated with specific details during the summer.

Allergies/dietary restrictions

We fully accommodate for allergies and dietary restrictions. Our registration form asks that you specify any dietary restrictions so that we can make sure your child is taken care of.

Alcohol?

No! We will not be providing alcohol.

Accessibility Services

Yes! Our registration form will have that option. We take it seriously, and every event is designed to be fully accessible. If you have any concerns, please email us at frosh@ncsc.ca, and we can go over our services in detail.

Students living off-campus

The majority of our leaders and executives also live off campus and would be more than willing to help your child navigate their way home. All event venues are also located near TTC stops to provide easy access to public transportation.
